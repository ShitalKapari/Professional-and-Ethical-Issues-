# Professional-and-Ethical-Issues-
This report is an individual coursework on Professional and Ethical Issues in Computing, focusing on the famous 2015 Jeep Cherokee hacking incident. It examines how connected automotive technologies, while improving convenience and performance, also introduce serious cybersecurity risks. The report begins with an introduction to Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA) and its Uconnect infotainment system, explaining how researchers exploited vulnerabilities to remotely control critical vehicle functions such as steering, braking, and transmission.

The analysis is structured around four major perspectives:

Social Issues – covering public safety risks, fear and anxiety among users, loss of trust in technology, privacy concerns, and the wider negative impact on society and the economy.

Ethical Issues – discussing unauthorized access, endangering human lives, responsible disclosure versus public exposure, negligence of manufacturers, and the misuse of technical knowledge.

Legal Issues – exploring unauthorized access under computer misuse laws, violation of vehicle safety regulations, product liability, negligence, and laws governing connected vehicles.

Professional Issues – highlighting the responsibilities of developers, adherence to codes of ethics (such as ACM), prioritizing public safety, accountability, transparency, and responsible vulnerability disclosure.

The report concludes with reflections on the importance of ethical hacking, legal accountability, and professional responsibility in designing secure connected systems. Overall, it demonstrates how a single cybersecurity incident can have social, ethical, legal, and professional implications, making it a valuable case study in understanding the risks of emerging technologies.
